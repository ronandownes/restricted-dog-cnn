01 · The catalyst

ImageNet changed what computer vision could become.

The breakthrough was not merely a new neural network. It was the combination of a huge labelled dataset, a demanding competition and enough compute to train deep models at scale.

1

Before ImageNet

Vision systems often depended heavily on engineered features. Researchers designed descriptors first, then trained a classifier on top.

Image→Hand-crafted features→Classifier
2

ImageNet + ILSVRC

A very large labelled image collection created a common arena where models could be compared on the same recognition task.

1,000classes in the classic ILSVRC classification task
3

What changed

Instead of manually defining the useful visual features, deep networks could learn increasingly abstract representations directly from images.

Pixels→Edges→Textures→Objects
The key ideaImageNet supplied the scale. CNNs supplied the representation learning. GPUs supplied the practical compute.

02 · CNN mechanics

How does a CNN actually see an image?

A CNN does not “see a dog” in one step. It repeatedly applies local learned filters and builds more useful feature representations layer by layer.

×
-101-101-101
→
Feature mapstrong response where the learned pattern is present
Convolution

Learn a local detector

A small kernel slides across the image. The same weights are reused everywhere, making CNNs efficient and naturally suited to spatial patterns.

Activation

Keep useful signals

Non-linear activations let stacked layers model far more complex patterns than a sequence of linear operations could.

Pooling / stride

Compress spatial detail

Later representations become smaller while retaining important signals, increasing the effective receptive field.

Hierarchy

Build features on features

Early layers respond to simple structure; deeper layers combine those responses into textures, parts and higher-level concepts.

03 · Architecture evolution

Every famous CNN solved a problem left by the previous generation.

Do not memorise architecture names. Memorise the design pressure that produced each one.

2012

AlexNet

Can deep CNNs win at scale?

GPU training, ReLU, dropout and data augmentation helped produce the ImageNet breakthrough.

2014

VGG

Can depth be made systematic?

Repeated small 3×3 convolutions created a clean, uniform architecture and showed the power of going deeper.

2014–15

Inception

Which spatial scale matters?

Parallel branches process information at several receptive-field sizes, combining them efficiently.

2015

ResNet

Why does deeper training degrade?

Skip connections let layers learn residual changes around an identity pathway, making much deeper networks trainable.

2016

Xception

Can convolution be factorised?

Depthwise-separable convolution separates spatial filtering from channel mixing, improving parameter efficiency.

2016

InceptionResNetV2

Can two strong ideas be combined?

Multi-scale Inception modules gain residual shortcuts, blending representational richness with easier optimisation.

2017

NASNet

Can machines search for architectures?

Neural architecture search shifts part of network design from hand-crafted engineering toward automated optimisation.

2020

Vision Transformer

Do images require convolution?

An image can be split into patches and processed as a sequence using Transformer self-attention.

08 · Explainability

Grad-CAM asks: where was the CNN looking?

It projects class-relevant activation back onto the image. That can expose useful visual focus, suspicious background reliance or ambiguous morphology—but it is not a causal explanation.

Interpretive boundaryGrad-CAM indicates regions associated with a prediction. It does not prove why the model decided, and it certainly does not establish behaviour, danger or legal identity.

09 · The next paradigm

If CNNs worked so well, why did Transformers enter vision?

CNNs build in strong assumptions: local connectivity and translation-friendly weight sharing. Transformers relax those assumptions and learn relationships between image patches through attention.

CNN

Locality is built in.

Pixels→Local filters→Feature hierarchy→Prediction

Excellent inductive bias for images; efficient learning of local spatial structure.

VS
ViT

Relationships are learned.

The important historical point: ViT did not prove that convolution was “wrong”. It showed that, given enough data and compute, a less image-specific architecture could become extremely competitive in vision.
